Team Mechronicles - T0B1 Through Time

2024, Programmer and Design Support

T0B1 Through Time is a third-person narrative shooter where you play as T0B1, a friendly robot who must research and defeat the mysterious Anachronist cult. Battle against the Anachronists with your independantly aimed dual blasters as you explore three distinct eras as you puzzle, platform, and fight your way through this time-traveling adventure. T0B1 Through Time was presented by Team Mechronicles at the DePaul School of Design's 2024 Opus Showcase.

Gossip World (WIP)

2024, Sole Creator

Gossip World is hardly a game - it is a digital fishtank that simulates the lives and drama of a procedurally generated friend group of self-serving socialites. The player takes a passive role as an audience to the evolving relationships between simulated, randomized actors who have been carefully designed to be shallow, scheming, and so, so in. Built in Unity for Art Games Workshop, lead by Professor Briank Schrank at DePaul, Gossip World is an experiment in storytelling and indirect narrative design, and an homage to the TV show Gossip Girl.

Ketchup Beat!

2023, Sole Creator

Ketchup Beat! is an extension for Ketchup Engine which adds rhythm game systems such as a song player with consistent timing and song synchronization, gameplay objects such as notes and lanes, and tools for reading rhythm game charts from a MIDI file. Throughout development, new features were also added to the base engine, such as an improved UI system and a new heightmap-based terrain system. Ketchup Engine

2023, Sole Creator

Ketchup is a 3D game engine developed by Haru Zemsky throughout the 2022-23 Winter and Spring quarters at DePaul's CDM. Built on CDM's Azul graphics library, Ketchup provides easy-to-use services for managing updating and drawing game objects, entities, and scenes. Ketchup also provides services for event-based mouse and keyboard input, single and repeating alarms and collision. Additionally, Ketchup provides the user with dev tools such as a visualizer for invisible objects, an on-screen log, and the KMath math library.

See the official documentation and tutorials (W.I.P.) for more details.
